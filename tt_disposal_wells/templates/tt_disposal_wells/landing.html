This map shows over 7,000 sites around Texas where wastewater, often from hydraulic fracturing operations, is getting disposed of in Texas. The amount of wastewater being disposed in Texas has skyrocketed with the spread of fracking, because it's cheaper to bury the water in disposal wells than to recycle it. Some of the disposal wells shown on this map are large commercial operations. Others are smaller, open only to oil and gas companies nearby.

The data used in this map comes from the Texas Railroad Commission, the agency regulating oil and gas production.
